Encoding Physics to Learn Reaction-diffiusion Processes

Oct 15, 2022 in Paper Notes

Abstract

a novel deep learning framework that forcibly encodes/preserves given physics structure to facilitate the learning of the spatiotemporal dynamics in sparse data regimes.

  • forward and inverse analysis of PDE systems
  • data-driven modeling
  • discovery of governing PDEs

Introduction

problems

  • laws of many systems remain uncertain or partially unknown
  • the computational cost of accurate numerical simulation is also prohibitive
  • the pure data-driven methods encounter the generalization issuse
  • the dominant physics-informed learning model generally represents a continuous learning paradigm
  • the discrete learning models, such as CNNs, GNNs and transformers alse have their disadvantages (a rich set of labeled data or given the explicit PDEs)

the proposed physics-encoded model

  1. discrete, hard encodes the physics structure into network architecture
  2. employs a unique convolutional network to capture the spatial patterns while time marching is performed by the recurrent unit
  3. incorporate well-known numerical time integration methods
